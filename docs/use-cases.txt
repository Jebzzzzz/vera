1. The Social Media "Headline Check" (Combating Viral Misinformation)
  The Scenario: A user is scrolling through social media and sees a highly sensational, emotionally charged headline or viral post that seems too crazy to be true (e.g., a controversial political quote or a shocking news event).

    The Action: The user copies the URL of the article or pastes the text of the post directly into the Vera app.

    The Result: Vera instantly decodes the chaos. It provides a straightforward "Fact Score" (e.g., Verified, False, or Missing Context) alongside three simple bullet points explaining the actual facts, stripped of the original post's bias or hyperbole.

2. The Group Chat "Rumor Filter" (Stopping the Spread of Hoaxes)
  The Scenario: A user receives a heavily forwarded message in a family or community group chat (like WhatsApp or Messenger). The message warns of a new local danger, a fake government policy, or a unverified health "cure," causing immediate anxiety.

    The Action: The user highlights the text message and shares or pastes it into Vera.

    The Result: Vera cross-references the text against known hoaxes, scams, and verified health/government databases. It immediately tells the user if this is a recycled internet rumor, allowing them to confidently reassure their family and stop the spread.

3. The Suspicious Link "Safety Check" (Preventing Phishing and Scams)
  The Scenario: A user receives an urgent text message or email claiming they have an unpaid toll, a blocked bank account, or an undelivered package, accompanied by a link. They are unsure if it is legitimate or a phishing attempt.

    The Action: The user copies the link and pastes it into Vera's search bar.

    The Result: Vera analyzes the URL's domain age, checks it against known scam registries, and verifies if it belongs to the actual institution. It delivers a clear "Safe" or "High Risk" verdict, saving the user from potential financial fraud and the stress of uncertainty.
